# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Engineers, tech learners, and potential collaborators discovering Luke through GitHub or referral. They arrive curious — either to read a specific article or to size up who built this. Context: desktop-first, they're not rushed, they're scanning for signal that this person is worth following.

## Product Purpose

Personal brand site for Luke, a backend engineer (Go, Java, Python) who writes about AI agents, cloud engineering, and frontend craft. The site is styled as a classified intelligence dossier on "HLM-1" (Human Large Model) — the conceit that Luke is himself a large model, the most dangerous entity in the hacker world. The site hosts: a technical blog, a frontend tutorial series (in progress), and project demos. Success: a visitor reads one post, subscribes, or reaches out.

## Brand Personality

Cyberpunk operator, technically authoritative, self-aware humor. The voice is deadpan classified-intel — dry, precise, never trying too hard. Three words: **classified, precise, alive**.

## Anti-references

- Generic developer portfolio (hero photo, skill badges, "contact me" form)
- Commercial SaaS landing pages (gradient hero, metric stats, "trusted by X companies")
- Crypto/web3 aesthetics (neon on pure black, excessive glow)
- Resume-site energy (objective statement, timeline, download CV)

## Design Principles

1. **The dossier is the person.** Every design choice should reinforce that you're reading a classified file, not a portfolio. If an element could exist on a normal blog, it should be redesigned.
2. **Rarity gives meaning.** Signature elements (corners, stamps, redacted fields) lose power when overused. Reserve them for the intelligence core — let the rest of the page breathe.
3. **Signal, don't shout.** High-saturation accents are category identifiers, not decoration. One channel per surface.
4. **Show the work.** Demos and writing are the proof. Navigation should get out of the way and surface the content fast.
5. **Bilingual without artifacts.** Chinese and English coexist on every page. System fonts only, no layout breaks at language boundaries.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA target. Supports prefers-reduced-motion (boot screen skips). Bilingual Chinese/English content throughout. No minimum requirement beyond reasonable contrast ratios.
