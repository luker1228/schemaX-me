# Never Too Late: Why I’m Restarting My English Journey Today

I have resolved to master English many times before. The first time was a decade ago during my university days. My roommate, Zongzhen Li, was exceptionally proficient in English—winning numerous awards and ranking number one in our school, which I deeply admired.

While I knew English offered distinct career advantages, I always struggled to stick with it. Currently, my reading skills are decent, but my listening and writing need significant improvement. In fact, my favorite English word used to be "very"—I relied on it constantly in my writing!

Today, I am making a firm commitment once again. I want a better career, and I know this skill is essential to boost my income. Age is just a number, and it is never too late to learn. From now on, I will dedicate an hour every day to this journey. After all, teaching is the best way to learn. By sharing my essays, I hope to inspire others while mastering the language myself.

### resolve to 

"Resolve to" is a stronger, more professional way to express a firm decision.

> After several unsuccessful job interviews, I resolve to master English and achieve fluency.

My roommate, Zongzhen Li, was exceptionally proficient in English—winning numerous awards and ranking number one in our school, which I deeply admired.

My colleague, James, was extremely talented at programming-mastering multiple languages and possessing a deep understanding of architecture, which deeply inspired me.
